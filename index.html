DeltAI — AI-Guided Scenario Planning for Disruptive Technologies

How will quantum computing, artificial intelligence, and robotics reshape your sector over the next 25 years? DeltAI turns that question into a structured, AI-powered group experience — no expertise required.

Centre for Quantum and Society
DeltAI
Exploring the future of Disruptive Technologies

Organisations across every sector are confronted by the same challenge: disruptive technologies — from quantum computing to AI to advanced robotics — are approaching practical deployment, yet their implications are difficult to think through in a concrete, structured way. Conventional strategy sessions rarely go far enough into the future, and the futures that are explored rarely feel grounded in real technological trajectories.

DeltAI is a facilitated scenario planning tool developed by the Centre for Quantum and Society. It guides groups through a set of AI-generated futures — from 2050 and working backwards to the near present — enabling teams to explore, discuss, and reflect on the long-term implications of disruptive technologies for their organisation.

How does it work?

A moderator sets up a session in minutes. Participants join on their own devices via a shared session code. The tool then leads the group through three time-horizon scenarios, each generated in real time by an AI model trained on sector-specific technology analyses.

Step 01
Configure the session

The moderator selects a sector and two disruptive technologies, then sets the scenario axes: resource outlook (abundance vs. limits) and system stability (stable vs. breaks). This takes under two minutes.

Step 02
Participants join on their devices

Viewers enter a four-character session code and their screens follow the moderator automatically — no app, no account, no friction.

Step 03
AI generates three futures

The tool produces scenarios for 2050+, 2030–2040, and 2025–2030, each grounded in the chosen technologies and sector context. Text streams in live, visible to all participants simultaneously.

Step 04
Reflect, intervene, and capture insights

The moderator guides group discussion at each time horizon. A final intervention prompt invites the group to propose a strategic response — and the AI generates the consequences of that choice.

Two ways to use DeltAI

DeltAI is designed to work in two distinct settings, each requiring a different level of preparation.

Mode 01
Self-service at events

DeltAI ships with pre-configured sector profiles across government, healthcare, critical infrastructure, finance, and culture. A facilitator can open the tool, choose a sector, and run a session in minutes — no setup required. Suited to conferences, lectures, or public events.

Mode 02
CQS-facilitated workshop

For deeper engagements, CQS prepares a custom organisation passport before the session — mapping the client's mission, stakeholders, and strategic priorities to the tool's technology analyses. The moderator arrives at a session that is already tailored to their organisation.

Why it matters

Quantum technologies, large-scale AI systems, and advanced robotics are no longer purely theoretical. Deployment timelines are compressing. Organisations that have not yet developed an intuition for these futures — what they might look like, what decisions they would require — risk being caught unprepared.

DeltAI does not predict the future. It makes futures thinkable. By anchoring group discussion in AI-generated scenarios rather than speculation, it allows teams to have concrete, productive conversations about long-term technological change — within a single workshop session.

The tool is freely available for non-commercial facilitation use. It was developed as part of the graduation project of Rixt Bijker at the Centre for Quantum and Society, TU Delft.
